3 · Moneyline (1X2) — Reading the Odds

The three outcomes are Home win, Draw, or Away win. Each shows a decimal odds number. Understanding what the number means is key:

The decimal odds is the total return multiplier per unit. So:
• 1.50 = 1.50× multiplier → 100 returns 150 (small gain, but the market sees this as very likely)
• 2.00 = 2.00× multiplier → 100 returns 200 (even-money, market sees ~50/50)
• 4.40 = 4.40× multiplier → 100 returns 440 (bigger return, but market sees it as less likely)
• 6.50 = 6.50× multiplier → 100 returns 650 (much bigger return, market thinks unlikely)

The rule: low number = likely outcome, smaller return. High number = unlikely outcome, bigger return. Implied probability ≈ 100 ÷ odds (so 1.50 ≈ 67%, 4.40 ≈ 23%, 6.50 ≈ 15%).

4 · Asian Handicap Cheat Sheet

Removes (or reduces) the draw by giving one team a head start. All AH bets settle on 90 minutes + injury time. Extra time and penalties don't count.

LineIf you back this team
0Win = win all · Draw = refund · Loss = lose all
−0.25Win by 1+ = win all · Draw = lose half · Loss = lose all
−0.5Win by 1+ goals · Draw or Loss = lose all
−0.75Win by 2+ = win · Win by 1 = win half
−1.0Win by 2+ · Win by 1 = refund
−1.5Win by 2+ · Anything else = lose
−2.0Win by 3+ · Win by 2 = refund
−2.5Win by 3+ · Else lose
Plus lines (+0.5, +1, etc) mirror the minus lines — team can lose by less than the line and still cash.
